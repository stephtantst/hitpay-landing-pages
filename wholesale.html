Frequently asked questions

Everything wholesale distributors and B2B trade businesses need to know about HitPay.

Does HitPay support high-value B2B invoices for wholesale trade?

Yes. HitPay Invoicing supports large B2B trade invoices with GST calculation, trade discounts, and partial payment terms — for example, 30% deposit upfront and balance on delivery. There is no cap on invoice amounts, making HitPay suitable for wholesale orders of any size. Automated reminders are sent before each due date to reduce overdue payments.

Can I collect payments from buyers in Malaysia, Philippines, and other APAC countries?

Yes. HitPay operates across 12 APAC markets including Singapore, Malaysia, Philippines, Indonesia, Thailand, Hong Kong, India, and more. Buyers pay in their preferred local method — DuitNow, GCash, GoPay, UPI — while you receive settlement in SGD. No foreign bank accounts or FX management required on your end.

Does HitPay integrate with Xero or QuickBooks for accounting?

Yes. HitPay integrates natively with both Xero and QuickBooks. Every invoice created and every payment received is automatically synced to your accounting system — eliminating manual data entry and making month-end reconciliation straightforward. This is particularly valuable for wholesale businesses managing hundreds of invoices per month.

How do automated invoice reminders work in HitPay?

When you create an invoice with a due date, HitPay automatically sends email reminders to your buyer at configured intervals — for example, 7 days before, 3 days before, and 1 day after the due date. This significantly reduces overdue invoices without any manual follow-up on your part, preserving your buyer relationships while improving your cash collection rate.

Can I set up recurring billing for weekly or monthly supply contracts?

Yes. HitPay's Recurring Billing supports weekly, fortnightly, monthly, and quarterly billing cycles with no cap on the recurring amount. Your buyer authorises the payment once and HitPay invoices and charges them automatically on your agreed schedule — ideal for regular supply agreements with retail chains, hotel groups, or restaurant chains.

My wholesale buyers consistently pay late — what can HitPay do to improve my collection rate?

HitPay's automated reminder system sends payment reminders at your configured intervals before and after the due date — without any manual effort. Research consistently shows that timely, professional reminders can improve on-time payment rates by 30–40%. You can also require a partial deposit before dispatching goods, reducing your financial exposure to late-payers. Together, these features significantly tighten your cash collection cycle without damaging buyer relationships.

Can I offer different payment terms to different wholesale buyers — NET 30 for some, NET 60 for others?

Yes. HitPay invoices are created individually per buyer, so you can set any due date you choose — NET 15, NET 30, NET 60, or a specific calendar date. For your most trusted long-term buyers, you can extend NET 60 terms. For newer accounts or higher-risk orders, you require a deposit or NET 15. Each buyer's full invoice history is tracked separately so you can review payment behaviour over time.

Does HitPay support purchase order (PO) reference numbers on invoices for corporate buyers?

Yes. HitPay Invoicing lets you add custom reference fields to each invoice — including your buyer's purchase order number, their internal cost centre code, or any other reference they need for their accounts payable workflow. This makes HitPay invoices compatible with large corporate and government procurement processes that require PO-to-invoice matching before releasing payment.

What is the most cost-effective way to collect SGD payments from Malaysian buyers?

For Malaysian buyers paying in SGD, PayNow is the most cost-effective at 0.4%. HitPay also supports DuitNow cross-border QR, which lets Malaysian buyers pay directly from their Malaysian banking app — settling in SGD on your end with no foreign bank account required. For very large invoices, bank transfer via HitPay Invoice is also supported. All options give you clean SGD settlement with no FX management on your side.

How long does it take to get set up on HitPay as a wholesale business?

Most wholesale businesses are verified and collecting payments within 1–2 business days. You sign up online, submit your ACRA registration and director NRIC, and HitPay's compliance team reviews your account. There are no lock-in contracts, no setup fees, and no minimum transaction volumes — so you can start sending invoices to buyers the moment your account is approved, with no ongoing commitment if your business model or volumes change.
