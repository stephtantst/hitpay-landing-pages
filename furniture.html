Frequently asked questions

Everything furniture stores and home furnishing businesses need to know about HitPay.

Does HitPay support BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later) for large furniture purchases?

Yes. HitPay integrates with Atome and GrabPay Later, allowing customers to split furniture purchases into 3 equal interest-free instalments. This is particularly effective for bedroom sets, dining tables, and sofas above S$1,000. Retailers who offer BNPL typically see higher average transaction values and fewer abandoned purchases due to sticker shock.

Can furniture showrooms use HitPay for both walk-in customers and online orders?

Yes. HitPay's POS system handles in-showroom sales while the HitPay Online Store lets customers browse and purchase from home. Inventory syncs automatically across both channels so you always have accurate stock levels. Multi-location showrooms can also manage separate inventory per location from the same dashboard.

How can I invoice interior design firms and contractors for trade orders?

HitPay Invoicing supports B2B trade invoices with GST calculation, trade discounts, and partial payment terms — such as 50% deposit upfront and balance upon delivery. Designers and contractors receive a professional GST-compliant invoice with a one-click payment button. Automatic reminders are sent before the due date so you don't need to follow up manually.

Does HitPay track inventory across multiple showroom locations?

Yes. HitPay's inventory management supports multiple locations. You can track stock levels per SKU at each showroom and manage transfers between branches from the same dashboard. Sales at any location automatically deduct from the correct location's inventory in real time.

What are HitPay's fees for furniture retailers?

HitPay charges 2.5% + S$0.30 for Visa/Mastercard, 0.4% for PayNow, and varying rates for Atome and GrabPay Later BNPL. There are no monthly platform fees and no setup costs — you only pay when a customer completes a purchase. For high-volume partners, custom rates may be available through the sales team.

My customers keep asking for instalment payments — how do I set up BNPL at my furniture store?

Once your HitPay account is active, Atome and GrabPay Later appear automatically as payment options at your POS and online store — no additional integration required. Customers choose to split into 3 interest-free instalments at checkout and you receive the full amount upfront from the BNPL provider. Setup takes minutes, not weeks. Furniture retailers that add BNPL typically see a measurable increase in average basket size as customers are more willing to consider higher-end pieces.

Can I use HitPay to accept payments at a trade show, pop-up event, or showroom roadshow?

Yes. HitPay's card terminal and Soundbox are portable — bring the device and your phone. At a trade show, you can accept card tap, PayNow QR, and generate a payment link on your phone for customers who want to pay a deposit on the spot. The Soundbox operates on its own SIM card so no Wi-Fi router is needed at the venue. This makes HitPay ideal for IKEA-style mega sales events, residential showroom launches, and outdoor pop-ups.

I sell to both retail walk-in customers and interior design trade accounts — can one HitPay account handle both?

Yes. One HitPay account handles both customer types. For retail walk-ins, use POS or the payment link with standard retail pricing. For trade accounts, use HitPay Invoicing with trade-specific pricing — custom line items, percentage trade discounts, and partial payment terms. All revenue from both customer types is tracked in a unified dashboard with no mixing of pricing or terms.

Does HitPay's online store support product variants like fabric colour, material, or size?

Yes. HitPay's Online Store supports product variants — you can list a sofa with options for fabric type (leather, microfibre, velvet), colour, and leg finish, each with its own price and stock count. Customers select their configuration before paying, and your inventory is deducted from the correct variant automatically. This is essential for furniture businesses where the same piece comes in multiple finishes and price points.

How does HitPay compare to using my bank's payment terminal for my furniture showroom?

Bank-issued terminals typically charge 2.5–3.5% per card transaction plus a fixed monthly rental of S$30–80. HitPay charges 2.5% + S$0.30 with no monthly rental — you own the terminal outright. More importantly, HitPay also accepts PayNow (0.4%), GrabPay, Atome BNPL, and Alipay+ — payment methods that traditional bank terminals don't support. Offering BNPL and PayNow alone can convert customers who would otherwise walk out without purchasing due to upfront cost hesitation.
