Frequently asked questions

Everything beauty and wellness businesses in Singapore, Malaysia, and the Philippines need to know about HitPay.

How do beauty salons and spas in Singapore collect deposits to reduce no-shows via PayNow?

Singapore beauty businesses create a HitPay Payment Link for a fixed deposit amount — for example, 50% of the treatment price — and share it via WhatsApp or Instagram when a client books. Clients pay via PayNow, GrabPay, or card instantly. No deposit, no confirmed appointment. This reduces no-shows because clients have financial commitment before the visit.

How do Malaysian salon owners collect booking deposits using DuitNow through HitPay?

Malaysian beauty businesses share a HitPay Payment Link that accepts DuitNow QR, FPX, Touch 'n Go eWallet, and Visa or Mastercard. Clients pay a deposit in seconds from their preferred app. Settlement is next business day in MYR. No separate QR terminal is needed — one payment link covers all Malaysian payment methods.

How do Philippine wellness studios collect GCash or Maya deposits for appointments using HitPay?

Philippine beauty businesses create a HitPay Payment Link accepting GCash, Maya, QR Ph, and Visa or Mastercard. Clients pay a deposit when they book online or via messaging apps. Settlement is next business day in PHP via InstaPay or PESONet. Businesses register with a valid SEC or DTI certificate and a government-issued ID.

Does HitPay support recurring monthly billing for spa memberships in Singapore, Malaysia, and the Philippines?

Yes. HitPay's Recurring Billing lets beauty businesses create membership plans and charge clients automatically each month — via PayNow and card in Singapore, DuitNow and Touch 'n Go in Malaysia, and GCash or Maya in the Philippines. No monthly subscription fee applies to HitPay for using this feature; businesses pay only the per-transaction rate.

Can beauty businesses in Singapore accept Atome or ShopBack PayLater BNPL for treatments?

Yes. Singapore wellness businesses can offer Atome and ShopBack PayLater through HitPay, allowing clients to split the cost of treatments or packages over 3 interest-free instalments. Malaysian salons can additionally offer Grab PayLater. This is especially popular for premium aesthetic treatments and package deals priced above S$300 or RM500.

Is HitPay suitable for solo beauty therapists and home-based salons in Southeast Asia?

Yes. HitPay has no monthly fees and no minimum transaction volume across Singapore, Malaysia, and the Philippines. Solo therapists use HitPay's free Payment Links to collect deposits via WhatsApp — no website, no POS terminal, and no upfront cost required. Businesses pay only a per-transaction rate set out at hitpayapp.com/pricing.

How do Malaysian beauty businesses sign up for HitPay?

Malaysian salons and wellness studios register with a valid SSM business registration number and the owner's MyKad (NRIC). Sole proprietors can apply with their personal MyKad. Approval typically takes 1–3 business days at no cost. Businesses then accept DuitNow QR, Touch 'n Go, FPX, and Visa or Mastercard immediately after approval.

What documents do Philippine beauty businesses need to sign up for HitPay?

Philippine wellness studios register with a valid SEC or DTI registration certificate plus a government-issued ID. Sole proprietors use their DTI registration; corporations present their SEC certificate. Approval takes 1–3 business days with no setup fees. Businesses can then accept GCash, Maya, QR Ph, and Visa or Mastercard immediately.

How do beauty businesses in Singapore sign up for HitPay?

Singapore salons and spas register with an ACRA business registration number. Sole proprietors and freelancers apply with their NRIC. No setup fees and no monthly subscription. Approval takes 1–3 business days. Businesses can then accept PayNow, GrabPay, Atome, and cards through Payment Links, the HitPay store, or an in-person terminal.

Can beauty businesses customise their HitPay payment page with their salon's logo and branding?

Yes. HitPay lets businesses customise payment pages with their name, logo, and brand colour across Singapore, Malaysia, and the Philippines. Clients see a professional branded page — not a generic portal — building trust for premium wellness and aesthetic businesses. Custom payment URLs like hitpay.me/luminspa are also available.

How do spas and salons issue a partial refund for a cancelled appointment through HitPay?

In the HitPay dashboard, locate the transaction and click Refund. Businesses issue a full or partial refund — for example, returning 50% if the client cancels within a grace window while retaining 50% as a no-show fee. Card refunds typically take 5–10 business days. PayNow, DuitNow, and GCash refunds are usually instant.

Does HitPay integrate with beauty booking platforms like SimplyBook.me or Fresha?

HitPay connects to booking platforms via Zapier or direct API integration. When a client books through SimplyBook.me or a custom booking flow, Zapier can trigger a HitPay deposit payment link automatically — creating a seamless book-and-pay workflow without any manual step for the salon owner.
