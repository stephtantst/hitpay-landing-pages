Frequently asked questions

Everything beauty salons, spas, and wellness businesses need to know about HitPay.

How can beauty salons and spas collect deposits to reduce no-shows?

With HitPay Payment Links, you create a deposit link — for example, 50% of the treatment price — and share it via WhatsApp or Instagram DM when a client books. They pay the deposit within minutes to confirm their appointment. No deposit, no confirmed slot. This dramatically reduces no-shows because clients have financial skin in the game.

Does HitPay support recurring monthly billing for spa memberships?

Yes. HitPay's Recurring Billing lets you create membership plans — for example, S$150/month for 2 facials — and clients subscribe with a single click via a shareable link. HitPay charges them automatically each month, retries failed payments with smart dunning, and sends receipts automatically. No manual intervention needed.

Can HitPay integrate with SimplyBook.me or Cal.com booking systems?

Yes. You can connect HitPay to SimplyBook.me or Cal.com via Zapier. When a client makes a booking, Zapier automatically triggers a HitPay deposit payment link to be sent — creating a seamless book-and-pay flow without any manual step. The booking is only confirmed once the deposit is received.

What payment methods can beauty and wellness clients use?

HitPay supports PayNow, Visa/Mastercard, GrabPay, Apple Pay, Google Pay, Atome (interest-free BNPL), and Alipay+. For in-salon payments, HitPay's card terminal accepts contactless, chip, and magnetic stripe payments. Offering multiple payment methods improves client convenience and reduces friction at checkout.

Is HitPay suitable for solo beauty therapists or home-based salons?

Yes. HitPay has no monthly fees and no minimum transaction volume. Solo therapists working from home or renting a chair can use HitPay's free Payment Links to collect deposits via WhatsApp — no website, no POS terminal, and no upfront cost required. You only pay a small per-transaction fee when you collect a payment.

I currently collect deposits via manual PayNow — how is HitPay different?

Manual PayNow requires you to share your UEN, wait for the transfer, check your banking app to confirm the right amount landed, then manually update your booking records. HitPay automates the entire flow: the client pays via a branded payment link, you receive an instant push notification with their name and amount, and the payment is logged in your dashboard automatically. It saves 10–15 minutes per booking and eliminates reconciliation errors at month-end.

Can I collect a booking deposit before the client has even visited my salon for the first time?

Yes — this is one of the most popular use cases. You create a deposit payment link and send it via WhatsApp the moment a new client enquires. They pay online from home before their first appointment, which filters out low-commitment enquiries and ensures every slot on your calendar represents a financially committed client. New client no-shows are virtually eliminated when a deposit is required upfront.

Does HitPay charge extra for the recurring billing or membership feature?

No. Recurring billing is included in your HitPay account at no additional monthly fee. You create membership plans, share the sign-up link, and HitPay handles all billing automatically. You only pay the standard per-transaction fee when a membership charge is processed — 2.5% + S$0.30 for card, or 0.4% for PayNow. Compare this to dedicated membership platforms that charge S$50–150/month before any member subscribes.

Can I brand my payment links with my salon's logo and name?

Yes. HitPay lets you customise your payment page with your business name, logo, and brand colour. When clients open your payment link, they see a professional page with your salon's branding — not a generic payment portal. Your payment URL can also be a memorable custom link like hitpay.me/luminspa. This builds trust and reinforces your brand identity throughout the payment experience, which matters especially for premium wellness and aesthetic businesses.

How do I issue a refund for a cancelled spa appointment through HitPay?

In your HitPay dashboard, locate the transaction and click Refund. You can issue a full refund or a partial refund — for example, returning 50% if the client cancels within your cancellation window while retaining the other 50% as a no-show fee. Card payment refunds typically take 5–10 business days. PayNow refunds are usually instant. Your refund policy is entirely your decision — HitPay simply executes what you initiate.
