Frequently asked questions

Everything computer and electronics retailers need to know about HitPay.

Does HitPay support BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later) for high-value computer purchases?

Yes. HitPay integrates with Atome and GrabPay Later, allowing customers to split purchases into 3 equal interest-free instalments. This is ideal for gaming PCs, professional workstations, and laptops above S$1,000. Offering BNPL typically increases average order value and reduces purchase hesitation for high-ticket items.

Can I use HitPay for both my physical store and my online website?

Yes. HitPay offers a unified POS system for in-store sales and an Online Store for web sales, with shared inventory management. You can also integrate HitPay with your existing Shopify or WooCommerce store via official plugins — supporting PayNow, GrabPay, Atome, and all major cards at checkout.

How does HitPay integrate with Shopify and WooCommerce?

HitPay offers official plugins for both Shopify and WooCommerce. Once installed, HitPay becomes a payment option at your website checkout — supporting PayNow, GrabPay, Atome, Visa/Mastercard, and Apple Pay. Orders and payment statuses sync automatically between your store and HitPay dashboard.

Can I send invoices to corporate clients for bulk hardware orders?

Yes. HitPay Invoicing supports B2B invoices with GST calculation, trade discounts, and partial payment terms. Corporate IT buyers can receive a professional, GST-compliant invoice for bulk PC or server orders and pay via PayNow or bank transfer with a single click — no manual bank detail exchange needed.

What are HitPay's transaction fees for electronics retailers?

HitPay charges 2.5% + S$0.30 for Visa/Mastercard, 0.4% for PayNow, and varying rates for BNPL providers. There are no monthly platform fees and no setup costs — you only pay when you make a sale. For high-volume partners, custom rates may be available through the sales team.

I'm currently using Stripe for my electronics store — why should I switch to HitPay?

Stripe doesn't natively support PayNow, GrabPay, or local BNPL providers like Atome without significant custom development. HitPay is built specifically for Singapore and Southeast Asia — with native PayNow (0.4%), GrabPay, Atome, and Alipay+ support out of the box, alongside a physical POS terminal for in-store sales. If your customers regularly ask for PayNow or instalment options, HitPay covers all channels that Stripe can't match locally without added complexity.

Can HitPay handle high-value transactions above S$10,000 for enterprise hardware orders?

Yes. HitPay supports high-value transactions with no artificial cap on individual invoice amounts. For large corporate PC, server, or AV equipment orders, businesses typically use HitPay Invoicing with PayNow or bank transfer as the payment method. For very large initial transactions, HitPay's compliance team may contact you as part of standard KYC procedures — this is standard practice for all regulated payment institutions.

Does HitPay support PayNow as a checkout option on my existing e-commerce website?

Yes. The HitPay WooCommerce and Shopify plugins include PayNow as a native checkout option. Customers scan a QR code on the checkout page and pay instantly from their banking app — no card entry, no 3DS friction. This is especially popular with Singapore customers who prefer PayNow for security and convenience, and can measurably improve checkout conversion rates versus card-only checkouts.

Is there a hardware cost or monthly rental fee for the HitPay card terminal?

HitPay's card terminal is a one-time hardware purchase — there is no monthly device rental or maintenance fee. You only pay a per-transaction rate when a sale is processed. The POS software itself runs on any iPad or Android tablet you already own, so there's no proprietary hardware lock-in. This is significantly more cost-effective than legacy terminal rental models that charge S$30–80/month before any transaction is made.

Can I run multiple store locations with separate inventory and revenue reporting?

Yes. HitPay POS supports multiple locations — you assign products to specific outlets, track inventory per location, and view revenue broken down by store in your dashboard. Stock deductions happen at the point-of-sale location in real time. This is particularly useful for computer retailers with flagship and satellite stores across different parts of Singapore, or for businesses with both a physical outlet and a warehouse showroom.
