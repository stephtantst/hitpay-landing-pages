Frequently asked questions

Everything travel agencies and tour operators need to know about HitPay.

What payment methods do APAC tourists prefer when booking tours in Singapore?

Chinese tourists prefer Alipay and WeChat Pay. Indian tourists commonly use UPI. Malaysians pay with Touch 'n Go and DuitNow, while Indonesians use GoPay and OVO. HitPay supports all of these through Alipay+ and regional wallet integrations — alongside PayNow, Visa/Mastercard, and GrabPay for local and corporate clients.

Can I collect a deposit upfront to confirm a tour booking?

Yes. HitPay Payment Links let you specify a deposit amount — for example, 30% of the tour package price — and share the link via WhatsApp or email. The client pays the deposit to confirm their booking and you invoice the balance separately closer to the departure date. The full payment trail is tracked in your HitPay dashboard.

Does HitPay support invoicing for group tour packages with multiple travellers?

Yes. HitPay Invoicing lets you itemise by traveller or service — accommodation, transfers, guided tours, meals — apply group discounts, and include GST. Clients receive a professional tax invoice with a one-click payment button supporting PayNow, card, or GrabPay. Automatic reminders are sent before the due date.

How quickly does HitPay settle payments to my bank account?

HitPay settles most payments the next business day (T+1). PayNow settlements can be near-instant depending on your bank. This fast settlement cycle is important for travel businesses managing guide fees, hotel pre-payments, and other operational costs between bookings.

Is HitPay free to use for travel agencies? What are the transaction fees?

HitPay charges no monthly fees and no setup fees. You pay only a per-transaction rate — 2.5% + S$0.30 for credit cards and 0.4% for PayNow. There is no minimum volume requirement or lock-in contract, making HitPay accessible for both small boutique agencies and large tour operators.

My current payment gateway doesn't support Alipay or WeChat Pay — can I switch to HitPay?

Yes. HitPay supports Alipay+, WeChat Pay, and 700+ payment methods across 12 APAC markets — including UPI, Touch 'n Go, DuitNow, GCash, and GoPay. Switching to HitPay involves no lock-in contract or early termination fee on HitPay's side. Many travel agencies switch specifically to unlock Chinese and APAC tourist wallet support that their previous gateway couldn't offer.

Can I use HitPay without a website to collect payment for tour bookings?

Yes. HitPay Payment Links work entirely without a website — you get a shareable URL you can send via WhatsApp, email, or SMS. You can also use your free HitPay storefront at hitpay.me/youragency to list tour packages publicly. No developer, no CMS, no monthly SaaS subscription is needed to start accepting payments.

How do I handle refunds or cancellations for tour payments made through HitPay?

HitPay's dashboard lets you issue full or partial refunds to the original payment method with a few clicks. For card payments, refunds typically appear within 5–10 business days. For PayNow, refunds are usually instant. Your cancellation and refund policy is entirely your own — HitPay simply processes the refund you initiate without imposing any platform-level cancellation rules.

Is HitPay regulated and safe for collecting large tour deposits?

Yes. HitPay is licensed by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) as a Major Payment Institution under the Payment Services Act, and is PCI DSS Level 1 certified — the highest standard for card data security. Over 15,000 Singapore businesses use HitPay to collect payments ranging from small retail transactions to tens of thousands of dollars in travel deposits.

How long does it take to set up HitPay and start collecting payments as a travel agency?

Most travel agencies are set up and collecting payments within 1–2 business days. You register online, submit your business documents (ACRA registration and director NRIC for Pte Ltd; personal NRIC for sole proprietors), and HitPay's compliance team verifies your account. Once approved, you can immediately create payment links and invoices — no hardware purchase is required to start accepting PayNow and card payments.
