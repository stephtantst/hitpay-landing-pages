Frequently asked questions

Everything F&B businesses in Singapore, Malaysia, and the Philippines need to know about HitPay.

How do F&B businesses in Singapore pay lower card rates with HitPay?

HitPay offers a preferential F&B card rate for qualifying restaurants and food businesses in Singapore — lower than the standard rate. PayNow costs 0.4% flat. There are no monthly fees and no setup fees, so businesses pay only when a customer pays them. See hitpayapp.com/pricing for current rates.

How do Malaysian café owners accept DuitNow, FPX, and Touch 'n Go through HitPay?

Malaysian F&B businesses can accept DuitNow QR, FPX bank transfers, Touch 'n Go eWallet, Boost, and GrabPay through HitPay's unified POS and payment links. All payment types are managed in one dashboard with MYR next-business-day settlement. No separate hardware is needed for each wallet — one QR handles them all.

How do restaurant owners in the Philippines collect GCash and Maya payments using HitPay?

Philippine F&B businesses accept GCash, Maya, QR Ph, and Visa/Mastercard through HitPay payment links, online stores, or card terminals. Settlement is next business day in PHP via InstaPay or PESONet. Businesses register using a valid BIR-registered entity (SEC or DTI), and approval typically takes 1–3 business days.

What is the HitPay Soundbox and how does it prevent fake PayNow screenshots at restaurants?

The HitPay Soundbox confirms PayNow payments directly with the payment network in real time, then announces the amount via audio alert — so confirmation comes from HitPay's system, not a customer-controlled screenshot. It runs on a built-in SIM card with no Wi-Fi needed, making it ideal for hawker stalls and outdoor food markets across Singapore.

How quickly do restaurant payment settlements reach a business bank account with HitPay?

HitPay settles most payments the next business day in Singapore (SGD), Malaysia (MYR), and the Philippines (PHP). Singapore merchants receive payouts via PayNow to their DBS, OCBC, UOB, or other local bank accounts. Malaysian merchants settle via Maybank, CIMB, or Public Bank. Philippine merchants settle via InstaPay or PESONet.

Can restaurants accept Alipay+ and WeChat Pay from Chinese tourists in Singapore and Malaysia?

Yes. HitPay supports Alipay+ and WeChat Pay in Singapore and Malaysia, allowing Chinese tourists to scan a single QR code and pay in CNY. HitPay handles the FX conversion automatically — merchants in Orchard Road, Clarke Quay (Singapore) or Pavilion, KLCC (Malaysia) receive local currency with no manual steps.

Does HitPay work at a hawker stall or kopitiam without Wi-Fi in Singapore?

Yes. The HitPay Soundbox connects via a built-in SIM card so it works anywhere with mobile coverage — no broadband required. Payment links and static QR codes work on a basic mobile data connection. Hundreds of hawker operators in Singapore run their entire payment setup with a Soundbox and no fixed internet infrastructure.

How do F&B businesses in Malaysia sign up for HitPay?

Malaysian restaurants and cafés register with a valid SSM business registration number and the owner's MyKad (NRIC). Sole proprietors using their personal MyKad can also apply. Approval typically takes 1–3 business days. There are no setup fees — businesses start accepting DuitNow QR, Touch 'n Go, and cards as soon as they are approved.

What documents do Philippine restaurant businesses need to sign up for HitPay?

Philippine F&B businesses register with a valid SEC or DTI registration certificate plus a government-issued ID. Sole proprietors use their DTI registration. Corporations present their SEC certificate. Approval takes 1–3 business days at no cost. Businesses can then accept GCash, Maya, QR Ph, Visa, and Mastercard immediately after approval.

How do restaurant businesses in Singapore sign up for HitPay?

Singapore F&B businesses register with an ACRA business registration number. Sole proprietors and freelancers can apply with their NRIC. There are no setup fees and no monthly subscription. Approval takes 1–3 business days, after which businesses can accept PayNow, GrabPay, Alipay+, and cards through the POS app, payment links, or static QR codes.

Does HitPay charge a monthly subscription fee for its restaurant POS software?

No. HitPay's POS software has no monthly subscription fee across Singapore, Malaysia, and the Philippines — businesses pay only a per-transaction rate. This is a significant cost advantage over restaurant POS systems that charge S$50–200/month or RM200–500/month in software fees before a single order is processed.

Can Malaysian F&B businesses accept Atome or GrabPay Later BNPL through HitPay?

Yes. Malaysian merchants can accept Atome, ShopBack PayLater, and Grab PayLater through HitPay, allowing customers to split purchases over instalments at no extra cost to the merchant beyond the standard BNPL rate. This is particularly popular for higher-value catering orders and premium dining experiences across Kuala Lumpur and Penang.
