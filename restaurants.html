Frequently asked questions

Everything restaurants, hawkers, and F&B businesses need to know about HitPay.

What is HitPay's transaction fee for F&B businesses?

HitPay offers a preferential F&B rate of 2% (compared to the standard 2.5%) for qualifying food and beverage merchants in Singapore. PayNow costs just 0.4% per transaction. There are no monthly fees and no setup fees — you only pay when a customer pays you.

Can my restaurant accept Alipay and WeChat Pay from Chinese tourists?

Yes. HitPay supports Alipay+ and WeChat Pay, allowing Chinese tourists to scan a QR code and pay instantly in their preferred app. HitPay also accepts UPI from Indian tourists, Touch 'n Go from Malaysians, and many other APAC wallets — making it ideal for restaurants in Orchard Road, Marina Bay, and other tourist-heavy areas.

What is the HitPay Soundbox and how does it work for restaurants?

The HitPay Soundbox is a countertop device that displays a PayNow QR code and announces each successful payment with a clear audio confirmation — eliminating the risk of fake PayNow screenshots from customers. It connects via built-in SIM card with no Wi-Fi needed, and is especially popular with hawker stalls and casual dining restaurants.

Does HitPay work with my existing restaurant POS system?

HitPay offers its own POS software that runs on iPad and Android tablets — so you may not need a separate system. For integration with third-party restaurant management systems, HitPay provides a REST API. Contact the HitPay sales team to discuss your specific integration requirements.

How quickly are restaurant payments settled to my bank account?

Most payments settle to your bank account the next business day (T+1). PayNow transactions can settle on the same day depending on your bank. This gives restaurants fast access to daily revenue for managing ingredient purchases, staff wages, and daily operations.

I'm currently using NETS or a bank terminal — how is HitPay cheaper and more capable?

HitPay's F&B card rate starts at 2% with no monthly terminal rental fee — you purchase hardware once and pay only per transaction. Traditional NETS and bank terminals typically charge S$30–80/month in device rental before any transaction. More importantly, HitPay also accepts Alipay+, WeChat Pay, GrabPay, Atome, and UPI — payment methods that legacy NETS terminals don't support, which are increasingly demanded by both tourist and local customers.

Can I use HitPay at a hawker stall or coffee shop without reliable Wi-Fi?

Yes. The HitPay Soundbox connects via its own built-in SIM card — not Wi-Fi — so it works wherever there's mobile network coverage, including hawker centres and outdoor food markets. For payment links and QR codes generated on your phone, a basic mobile data connection is sufficient. Many hawker operators use the Soundbox as their entire payment setup without any broadband infrastructure.

Does HitPay charge a monthly subscription fee for its restaurant POS software?

No. HitPay's POS software has no monthly subscription fee — you pay only a per-transaction rate when a sale is made. This is a significant advantage over restaurant-specific POS systems that charge S$50–200/month in software fees before a single order is processed. For a small café or hawker doing S$10,000/month in revenue, eliminating a S$100/month POS fee saves S$1,200/year.

How does the HitPay Soundbox prevent fake PayNow payment screenshots?

The Soundbox confirms payment directly with the payment network in real time and announces "Payment received: $24.50" the moment the transaction clears — not when the customer shows you a screenshot. Because the confirmation comes from HitPay's system rather than a customer-controlled screenshot, there's no opportunity for fraud. This is why the Soundbox has become the standard for hawker stalls and quick-service restaurants where verifying screenshots manually is impractical during peak hours.

Can I accept GrabFood and Foodpanda delivery payments through HitPay?

GrabFood and Foodpanda operate closed payment systems for their delivery orders, so those cannot be routed through HitPay. However, HitPay handles all your direct customer payments — dine-in, takeaway, phone orders, and catering bookings — including PayNow, cards, GrabPay (in-person wallet), Alipay+, WeChat Pay, and Atome. Using HitPay for direct orders alongside delivery platforms gives you the best of both channels with a single dashboard for all your self-collected revenue.
