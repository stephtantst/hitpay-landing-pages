Frequently asked questions

Everything gyms, yoga studios, and fitness businesses in Singapore, Malaysia, and the Philippines need to know about HitPay.

What is HitPay and how does it work for gym and fitness studio businesses?

HitPay is a payment platform that lets gyms, yoga studios, CrossFit boxes, and personal trainers in Singapore, Malaysia, and the Philippines automate membership billing, sell class passes, and accept walk-in payments. Fitness businesses pay zero monthly fees and receive next business day settlement in SGD, MYR, or PHP respectively.

How much does HitPay cost for gym and fitness studio businesses?

HitPay charges zero monthly fees and zero setup fees for fitness businesses. Pricing is per transaction only — visit hitpayapp.com/pricing for current card and wallet rates. There is no minimum transaction volume and no minimum member count, making HitPay accessible for studios of all sizes from day one.

How long does it take to set up HitPay for a gym or fitness studio?

Fitness businesses sign up for free at hitpayapp.com and submit business registration documents. Account approval takes 1–3 business days. After approval, creating membership plans and sending sign-up links to existing members typically takes under one hour. Most gym owners complete full member migration within a week.

What support does HitPay provide to fitness businesses?

HitPay provides live chat support, email support, and a self-serve help centre at support.hitpayapp.com. Fitness businesses can contact the sales team for onboarding assistance. Enterprise gym groups and franchise operators can access a dedicated account manager. Support is available in English across Singapore, Malaysia, and the Philippines.

How do gyms in Singapore automate monthly membership billing with PayNow?

Singapore gyms create membership plans in HitPay and share a subscription sign-up link with each member. Members authorise the first PayNow charge, and HitPay charges them every month on their renewal date — sending receipts and retrying any failed payments. The gym owner sees all active members and MRR in one dashboard without manual follow-up.

How do fitness studios in Malaysia collect recurring fees via DuitNow or FPX?

Malaysian fitness studios use HitPay Recurring Billing to charge members via FPX bank transfer or DuitNow QR each month. Members sign up once through a shareable link, and HitPay handles all subsequent charges automatically. Touch 'n Go eWallet and Grab PayLater are also accepted for class passes and one-off merchandise purchases.

How can CrossFit boxes and yoga studios in the Philippines accept GCash for class payments?

Philippine fitness studios connect GCash and Maya through HitPay to accept class payments and memberships. QR Ph is also available for instant bank transfers. Fitness businesses registered with DTI or SEC can apply for HitPay Philippines (OPSCOR-2023-0006, BSP-regulated) and receive PHP payouts the next business day.

What documents do fitness businesses need to sign up for HitPay?

Singapore fitness businesses need their ACRA business registration or NRIC for sole proprietors. Malaysian studios need SSM registration or MyKad for sole proprietors. Philippine gyms need DTI or SEC registration and a valid government-issued ID. All applicants must provide a local bank account for settlement in their respective currency.

What happens when a gym member's recurring payment fails?

HitPay retries failed membership payments automatically using smart dunning logic — attempting the charge again at optimised intervals to maximise collection. Members receive an automated email to update their payment method. The gym can see all failed attempts, retry outcomes, and outstanding balances in the dashboard — no manual follow-up required.

Can gym members manage or pause their own subscriptions?

Yes. HitPay provides a member self-service portal where members update their payment method, view billing history, pause, or cancel their membership — without contacting the gym. This is particularly useful for members in Singapore, Malaysia, and the Philippines who take seasonal breaks or travel frequently between markets.

Can fitness studios sell class passes via WhatsApp without a website?

Yes. HitPay Payment Links let fitness studios create a class-specific payment link in under 60 seconds and share it via WhatsApp, Instagram Stories, or link-in-bio. Clients pay online to confirm their spot. Studios set quantity limits so a HIIT class link closes automatically once all spots are filled — no website required.

Does HitPay integrate with Mindbody or Glofox gym management software?

HitPay connects to Mindbody, Glofox, and other fitness management platforms via Zapier automations — for example, triggering a subscription sign-up link when a new member is created in the booking system. HitPay's REST API supports deeper custom integrations. Contact the HitPay sales team for enterprise integration guidance.

Is HitPay suitable for small yoga or pilates studios with fewer than 50 members?

HitPay has no monthly platform fee and no minimum member count. A studio with 10 members pays the same low per-transaction rate as one with 500 members. This makes HitPay practical from day one for boutique yoga studios, reformer pilates studios, and personal trainers building their first client base in Singapore, Malaysia, or the Philippines.

Can gyms sell supplements and merchandise through the same HitPay account?

Yes. HitPay's built-in Online Store allows fitness businesses to sell protein supplements, branded apparel, accessories, and class passes alongside memberships from one dashboard. In-person merchandise sold at reception via HitPay POS is tracked in the same inventory and reporting system, giving a unified view of all revenue streams.

Can fitness businesses in Malaysia accept Touch 'n Go or Atome for memberships?

Yes. Malaysian fitness businesses using HitPay accept Touch 'n Go eWallet, Grab PayLater, and Atome buy-now-pay-later — in addition to DuitNow QR and FPX. Offering BNPL options like Atome lets studios spread membership costs over instalments, which can increase conversion rates for higher-tier plans priced above RM200/month.

How do fitness businesses receive their payouts after accepting payments?

HitPay settles funds to the fitness business's bank account the next business day in SGD for Singapore, MYR for Malaysia, and PHP for the Philippines — with no manual withdrawal required. This predictable payout schedule helps gym owners plan for monthly expenses like rent, payroll, and equipment leases.
