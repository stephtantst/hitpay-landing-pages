Frequently asked questions

Everything Singapore businesses need to know about HitPay's multi-currency virtual account and global collections.

What is HitPay's multi-currency virtual account and how does it work?

HitPay's multi-currency virtual account is a DBS-backed receiving account that allows Singapore businesses to collect payments in 13 currencies from local and overseas business partners alike. Each business receives a unique account number and SWIFT/BIC details. SGD transfers from local Singapore partners arrive in real time via FAST and PayNow. All other currencies — USD, EUR, GBP, AUD, HKD, and more — arrive via SWIFT from overseas counterparties. Incoming funds appear in the same HitPay dashboard alongside local and cross-border customer payment collections.

Which currencies can a Singapore business receive through HitPay's virtual account?

HitPay's multi-currency virtual account currently supports receiving in 13 currencies. SGD is the only currency with a local instant rail — payments arrive in real time via FAST or PayNow. USD, EUR, GBP, AUD, HKD, and other supported currencies arrive via SWIFT wire, typically within 1–3 business days. Local EUR, GBP, and AUD rails via SEPA, Faster Payments, and NPP are in development. Local PHP support via InstaPay and PESONet is planned.

How long does it take for SGD payments to arrive in a HitPay virtual account?

SGD transfers from Singapore senders arrive in real time when sent via FAST or PayNow to a HitPay virtual account. Other Singapore bank transfers via GIRO settle within one business day. USD, EUR, GBP, AUD, and other foreign currencies arrive via SWIFT, with standard international wire transit times of 1–3 business days depending on the sending bank and country.

How does HitPay's virtual account differ from other multi-currency platforms?

HitPay is the only platform that unifies C2B collections — cards, PayNow, GrabPay, QR — with B2B virtual account receiving in one dashboard, backed by DBS Bank rather than an e-money institution. Most multi-currency platforms focus on international transfers only and offer broader local-rail coverage at this stage, but none can collect from a merchant's customers via card or wallet, and none operate on a bank-grade rail backed by DBS. For Singapore SMEs that also process customer payments, HitPay removes the need for a second platform entirely.

Is money held in HitPay's virtual account safe — what bank backs it?

Funds in HitPay's multi-currency virtual account are held at DBS Bank — Singapore's largest bank by assets. DBS is a fully licensed bank, not an e-money institution. The primary risk associated with EMI-backed fintech accounts is fund freezes with limited recourse. DBS-backing means the underlying custodian operates under full banking supervision. Each account is issued in the business's own name, not held in a pooled float.

Can a Singapore business receive USD payments from overseas clients through HitPay?

Yes. Singapore businesses can receive USD payments from overseas clients via SWIFT wire transfer into their HitPay DBS-backed virtual account. USD funds arrive within 1–3 business days and are displayed in the HitPay dashboard. HitPay is developing local USD receiving via ACH and Fedwire details — allowing US clients to send domestic bank transfers instead of international wires. This feature is in active development and not yet available.

What FX conversion rate does HitPay charge on the virtual account?

HitPay charges a 1% markup over the interbank mid-rate for foreign currency conversion — for example, converting a USD or EUR balance to SGD. Most inbound currency receipts carry no collection fee. SGD collection via FAST or PayNow costs S$0.50 per collection; AUD collection costs A$3.00 per collection. There are no monthly account fees and no setup fees.

Does a business need to open a new account to use HitPay's virtual account?

No. HitPay's multi-currency virtual account is available inside an existing HitPay account. Businesses already using HitPay for card, PayNow, or e-wallet collections can activate multi-currency receiving from their existing dashboard — no new vendor, no second KYB process, no additional hardware. New businesses complete standard ACRA-based KYB once and gain access to both customer payment tools and the virtual account simultaneously.

What types of Singapore businesses can open a HitPay virtual account?

Singapore-registered businesses and sole proprietors can open a HitPay virtual account. Eligible entities include private limited companies (Pte. Ltd.), limited liability partnerships, sole proprietors, and partnerships registered with ACRA. Common users include exporters, professional service firms, digital agencies, SaaS companies, wholesale distributors, and any Singapore business that invoices local or overseas clients and needs a structured way to receive SGD FAST transfers from local partners or international bank transfers from overseas counterparties, alongside local and cross-border customer payments.

How does HitPay reconcile customer payments and overseas business receivables together?

HitPay's dashboard displays all payment types — local and cross-border customer card, PayNow, e-wallet transactions, local SGD FAST transfers from business partners, and overseas SWIFT receipts — in a unified transaction ledger with settlement and fee detail for each. Businesses can export reconciliation reports covering C2B customer payments and all B2B business receivables (local and overseas) in a single CSV. Xero and QuickBooks integrations are available to sync all transactions automatically, including GST-ready categorisation for Singapore tax reporting.

What documents are needed to open a HitPay virtual account in Singapore?

To open a HitPay virtual account in Singapore, a business provides its ACRA UEN, the company director's or authorised representative's NRIC or passport, and a valid Singapore business bank account. Sole proprietors apply with their NRIC and MyInfo. Sign-up is fully online and approval typically takes 1–3 business days. There are no setup fees and no monthly account fees.

What currencies will HitPay's virtual account add local rail support for in the future?

HitPay is developing local rail support for EUR via SEPA, GBP via Faster Payments, and AUD via NPP — enabling European and Australian clients to send domestic bank transfers instead of international SWIFT wires. Local PHP receiving via InstaPay and PESONet is planned, which would be a differentiated capability with no equivalent from most global competitors. Local USD receiving via ACH and Fedwire is also in active development. No specific dates have been announced.
